# Career Match Evaluation Rubric

## Overview

The Career Match Evaluation rubric assesses how well a generated CV matches the target job description. It scores the CV based on several criteria, each contributing to an overall score out of 100.

## Rubric Criteria

### 1. **Experience Relevance** (20 points)
This criterion evaluates how closely the professional experience in the CV aligns with the responsibilities and requirements outlined in the job description.

- **Score Range**: 0–20
- **Feedback**: Feedback will include whether the experience section highlights the most relevant skills and job responsibilities for the target role.

### 2. **Skill Match** (20 points)
Assesses how well the skills and certifications listed in the CV match the skills required for the job.

- **Score Range**: 0–20
- **Feedback**: Provides feedback on any missing or irrelevant skills.

### 3. **Education Suitability** (20 points)
Evaluates whether the education history in the CV meets the requirements of the target position.

- **Score Range**: 0–20
- **Feedback**: Includes suggestions for enhancing the education section if necessary.

### 4. **Formatting Quality** (20 points)
Checks whether the CV is formatted in a professional, ATS-friendly manner, following standard industry practices.

- **Score Range**: 0–20
- **Feedback**: Includes suggestions for improving readability and visual appeal.

### 5. **Professional Tone** (20 points)
Evaluates the language and tone of the CV, ensuring it maintains a professional and consistent voice.

- **Score Range**: 0–20
- **Feedback**: Provides feedback on tone, grammar, and professionalism.

## Overall Evaluation

The total score is out of 100. The final evaluation provides a summary of strengths and areas for improvement.
